STANISLAV PETROV the man who saved humanity

Averting a possible nuclear war at the peak of the cold war:

The record on how close humanity came to a full-blown nuclear war is insufficiently recognized, historians thought that a full blown nuclear war during the 1980’s cold war between the United States and the Soviet Union could have wiped out the northern hemisphere, but new studies show that it was in fact much more dangerous than that. In 1983 during the war crises, Russia automated early warning systems detected an incoming missile strike From the United States, sending the highest-level alert. The protocol for Russian military was to retaliate immediately with a nuclear attack on its own. There was an officer, who decided to disobey the orders and not report the warnings. Stanislav Petrov was a lieutenant colonel in the Soviet air defence forces and duty officer at Serpukhov-15, he decided to report the alert as a system malfunction.

“I had a funny feeling in my gut,” he told the Washington Post in 1999, “I didn’t want to make a mistake. I made a decision, and that was it.”

Stanislav died at the age of 77, Thanks to his brave decision we are alive today to talk about it.
